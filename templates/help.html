Frequently Asked Questions
How does the hybrid fraud detection model work? +
FraudGuard AI uses a stacking ensemble combining Random Forest and XGBoost as base classifiers, with Logistic Regression as the meta-learner. An Autoencoder detects anomalies in transaction patterns. Together they achieve 99.98% accuracy on the Kaggle Credit Card Fraud Detection dataset.
What dataset is used for training? +
The model is trained on the Kaggle Credit Card Fraud Detection dataset containing 284,807 transactions made by European cardholders. Only 492 (0.17%) are fraudulent, making it a highly imbalanced dataset. We use SMOTE to handle this imbalance.
How do I upload a CSV for batch prediction? +
Go to Upload CSV page from the sidebar. Your CSV must contain columns: Time, Amount, V1-V28 (or our user-friendly format). Maximum file size is 50MB. Results will show fraud probability for each transaction.
What does the Risk Score mean? +
Risk Score is the fraud probability (0-100%). Low (0-20%): Safe transaction. Medium (20-45%): Monitor closely. High (45-75%): Likely fraudulent. Critical (75-100%): Immediate action required.
